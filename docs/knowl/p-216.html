The complement \(\overline{G}\) of graph \(G\) is the "graph of edges missing in \(G\text{.}\)" It has the same vertices as \(G\text{.}\) But the edge \((u,v) \in E(\overline{G})\) if and only if \((u,v) \notin E(G)\text{.}\) Draw the complement \(\overline{G}\) of the graph \(G\text{.}\) Can you draw \(\overline{G}\) so that none of its edges are crossing?

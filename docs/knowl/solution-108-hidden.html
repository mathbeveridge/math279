Use \((\star)\) to show that \(5^{k+1}-1= 4\ell\) for some integer \(\ell\text{.}\)

Using the hint, we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} 5^{k+1}-1 &=& 5^{k+1}-1 + 5^k - 5^k \\ &=& (5^{k+1} - 5^k) + (5^k-1) \\ &=& 5^k(5-1) + (5^k-1) \\ &=& 5^k \cdot 4 + (5^k-1) \\ &=& 5^k \cdot 4 + 4r \mbox{by } (\star) \\ &=& 4 (5^k + r) \end{array} \end{equation*}

and so \(5^{k+1}-1\) is divisible by 4. (Namely: \(5^{k+1}-1 = 4 \ell\) where \(\ell = 5^k + r\text{.}\))

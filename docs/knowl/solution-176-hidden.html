Let \(A_2\text{,}\) \(A_5\) and \(A_7\) be the set of numbers divisible by 2, 5, and 7, respectively. We have

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc} |A_2| = 70 & |A_5| = 28 & |A_7| = 20 \\ |A_2 \cap A_5| = 14 & |A_2 \cap A_7| = 10 & |A_5 \cap A_7| = 4 \\ &|A_2 \cap A_5 \cap A_7| = 2 \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}

By inclusion-exclusion the answer is

\begin{equation*} 70+28+20 - 14 - 10 - 4 + 2 = 92 \end{equation*}

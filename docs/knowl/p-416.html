Using the hint, we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} 5^{n+1}-1 &=& 5^{n+1}-1 + 5^n - 5^n \\ &=& (5^{n+1} - 5^n) + (5^n-1) \\ &=& 5^n(5-1) + (5^n-1) \\ &=& 5^n \cdot 4 + (5^n-1) \\ &=& 5^n \cdot 4 + 4k \mbox{by } (\star) \\ &=& 4 (5^n + k) \end{array} \end{equation*}

and so \(5^{n+1}-1\) is divisible by 4. (Namely: \(5^{n+1}-1 = 4 \ell\) where \(\ell = 5^n + k\text{.}\))

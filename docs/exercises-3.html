Exercises 3.2 Practice Problems

1. Two Truth Tables.

  1. Fill in this truth table. \[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c} P & Q & P \wedge Q & \neg (P \wedge Q) \\ \hline T & T & & \\ T & F & & \\ F & T & & \\ F & F & & \\ \end{array} \] Solution.

    \[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c} P & Q & P \wedge Q & \neg (P \wedge Q) \\ \hline T & T & T& F\\ T & F & F & T\\ F & T & F & T\\ F & F & F & T\\ \end{array} \]

  2. Now fill in this truth table. \[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c} P & Q & \neg P & \neg Q & \neg P \vee \neg Q \\ \hline T & T & & & \\ T & F & & & \\ F & T & & & \\ F & F & & & \\ \end{array} \] Solution.

    \[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c} P & Q & \neg P & \neg Q & \neg P \vee \neg Q \\ \hline T & T & F& F& F \\ T & F & F& T & T\\ F & T & T& F& T\\ F & F & T& T& T \\ \end{array} \]

  3. Look at the last column of each of the two tables above. What do you conclude? Write a mathematical statement (using symbols) that summarizes your observation. Solution.

    These two statements are equivalent. In other words, \[ \neg (P \wedge Q) \Longleftrightarrow \neg P \vee \neg Q. \]

2. Truth Table for \(\neg (P \vee Q)\).

Fill in the following truth table. Using the previous problems as inspiration, find another compound statement that is equivalent to \(\neg (P \vee Q)\text{.}\) Add columns to your truth table to show that you are correct. \[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c} P & Q & P \vee Q & \neg (P \vee Q) \\ \hline T & T & & \\ T & F & & \\ F & T & & \\ F & F & & \\ \end{array} \] Solution.

\[ \​begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c} P & Q & P \vee Q & \neg (P \vee Q) & \neg P & \neg Q & \neg P \wedge \neg Q \\ \hline T & T & T & F & F & F & F \\ T & F & T & F & F & T & F \\ F & T & T & F & T & F & F\\ F & F & F & T & T & T & T \\ \end{array} \]

3. The Effect of Negation.

Look back at the last two problems. What is the effect of negation on the AND operator? The OR operator? Solution.

Under negation, AND becomes OR, while OR becomes AND.
